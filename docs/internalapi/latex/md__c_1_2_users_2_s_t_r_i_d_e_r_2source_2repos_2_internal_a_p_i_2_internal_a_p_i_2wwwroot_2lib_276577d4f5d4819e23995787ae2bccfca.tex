Returns a function that ensures that {\ttfamily fn} is only called {\itshape after} {\ttfamily delay} milliseconds have elapsed. When the returned function is called before the delay has elapsed, it will wait until the delay has elapsed and then call {\ttfamily fn}. When the returned function is called after the delay has elapsed, it will call {\ttfamily fn} after the next "{}tick"{} (it will always be called asynchronously). The context and arguments that the returned function is called in are applied to {\ttfamily fn}.

In the below example {\ttfamily on\+Loaded} will not be executed before a 1000 millisecond delay. Even if {\ttfamily load\+Images} loads and calls {\ttfamily callback} earlier. However, say the images take 1500 milliseconds to load, it will trigger {\ttfamily on\+Loaded} immediately.


\begin{DoxyCode}{0}

\DoxyCodeLine{loadImages(callback);}
